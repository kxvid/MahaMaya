## Legal Name vs. Public Name

| | |
|---|---|
| **Legal entity** | Mahakala LLC *(file in CA as your preferred honoring of the goddess)* |
| **DBA (public-facing)** | Kala Studios |
| **Domain** | kalastudio.ai |

The legal name honors the Sanskrit root and personal heritage. The DBA is what appears on the site, emails, contracts (as "d/b/a Kala Studios"), invoices, LinkedIn, and Stripe. Foreign clients get a short, pronounceable name. Paperwork keeps the meaning intact.

**Action items:** File DBA in your county. Add "Mahakala LLC d/b/a Kala Studios" to your contract template, Stripe account, and footer fine print only. Lead copy always uses "Kala Studios."

## Etymology (for when someone asks)

"Kala" (Sanskrit) means art, skill, and the generative force of creation. The 64 Kalās are the classical disciplines: architecture, design, logic, mathematics, music, craft. The name was chosen because this work sits at that intersection. Aesthetic craft meets technical precision.

Keep this ready as a one-liner. Don't lead with it. Let the work speak first.

## Voice: Three DOs

1. **Helpful, not hawkish.** We're offering a makeover, not a warning.
2. **Specific, not salesy.** Name the thing. Show the result. No hype.
3. **Calm confidence.** Short sentences. Plain words. Let the design speak.

## Voice: Three DON'Ts

1. **No fear or scarcity.** No "losing leads," "falling behind," "spots running out," countdowns, or pulsing warning dots.
2. **No em-dashes.** Periods and commas only.
3. **No unearned jargon.** No "synergy," "10x," "crushing it," "growth hacks," "game-changer."

## The Cross-Cultural Test

Before anything ships externally, ask:

1. Would a small-business owner in Pasadena read this and feel welcomed?
2. Would a founder in Singapore understand it without a dictionary?
3. Would I send this to a friend who runs a business?

If any answer is "no," rewrite until all three are "yes."
